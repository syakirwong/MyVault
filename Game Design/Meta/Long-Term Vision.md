# Long-Term Vision

---

## Phase 1: Launch (MVP)

- 6 Origins, 2 regions, core gem system
- Establish build experimentation as core loop
- Prove economy model works
- See [[MVP Design]]

---

## Phase 2: Expansion 1 (Month 4-6)

- Add 2 new Origins + 1 new gem domain
- Add Verdant Depths + Obsidian Wastes regions
- 4 new dungeons
- First raid (10-player)
- Guild system expansion (halls, alignment)
- 8 new skill gems, 12 new modifier gems
- Alignment depth expanded to 60

---

## Phase 3: Expansion 2 (Month 8-12)

- Add 1 new Origin + 1 new gem domain
- Add Celestial Peaks region
- Naval combat system
- Ship customization
- Trade routes
- Second raid
- Creator ecosystem full launch (leaderboards, rewards)
- 6 new skill gems, 10 new modifier gems
- Alignment depth expanded to 100

---

## Phase 4: Living World (Year 2+)

- Seasonal content rotation
- Community-driven balance (creator input on meta)
- Player housing
- Cross-server events
- Competitive esports support (arena tournaments)
- Modding tools for community content (cosmetic only)
- Continuous gem additions (target: 50+ skill gems, 100+ modifier gems)

---

## Long-Term Identity

The game becomes a PLATFORM for build creativity:

- Players are creators, not just consumers.
- The meta shifts through player innovation, not just developer patches.
- Community theorycrafting drives engagement.
- Build culture (sharing, competing, iterating) is the endgame.

---

## Revenue Sustainability

- Seasonal battle passes provide recurring revenue.
- Cosmetic releases tied to content expansions.
- Creator ecosystem drives organic marketing (build videos, guides).
- Competitive scene attracts viewers and sponsors.

# Progression Systems

## Progression Philosophy

**Horizontal over vertical.** Players get MORE OPTIONS, not just bigger numbers.

A level 50 player with 100 hours should not be strictly "stronger" than a level 50 player with 20 hours. They should have more tools, more mastered gems, more build flexibility — but not an insurmountable stat advantage. Power comes from knowledge and creativity, not time invested.

## Character Level (Soft Cap)

- **Level 1-50:** Tutorial and gem domain mastery (fast, approximately 20 hours of play)
- **Level 50:** Soft cap — all content is accessible at this point
- **Level 50-60:** Prestige levels granting cosmetic titles and minor stat bonuses (less than 5%)
- **No infinite scaling** — there is a ceiling, and it is reachable

The level curve is designed so that reaching the "real game" does not feel like a job. The journey to 50 teaches gem domain mechanics, introduces the [[Gem System]], and walks players through each content type.

## Gem Mastery

Each gem has mastery levels 1-10:

- **Level 1-3:** Unlock base modifiers
- **Level 4-6:** Unlock advanced modifier slots
- **Level 7-9:** Unlock visual evolution stages
- **Level 10:** Signature visual effect + can teach the gem to other players

Mastery is gained through USE, not through grinding mobs. Using a gem in diverse content (PvE, PvP, crafting support) accelerates mastery. A player who only farms the same dungeon will master gems slower than one who engages with the full breadth of content.

See [[Gem System]] for full gem mechanics and [[Skill Gems Reference]] and [[Modifier Gems Reference]] for the complete gem catalog.

## Build Mastery

When a full build (Origin + gem loadout + alignment) is used consistently:

- The build earns a **mastery rating**
- Higher mastery grants **visual polish** on the build's signature effects (particles become more refined, animations smoother)
- Published builds on the [[Creator Ecosystem]] display the creator's mastery level as a trust signal

Build mastery encourages commitment to a playstyle while still allowing experimentation — mastery is per-build, not per-character.

## Alignment Progression

- Alignment points are earned through gameplay choices, not grinding
- Deep alignment unlocks **exclusive gems** and **visual effects** tied to that alignment's theme
- Switching alignments costs more the deeper a player has committed (see [[Alignment System]] for full details)

Alignment is the primary narrative-mechanical bridge. It shapes what a player can do AND who they are in the world.

## Gear Progression

- Gear provides **BASE STATS ONLY** (defense, HP, resource pool)
- Gear does **NOT** provide skill power — that comes entirely from the [[Gem System]]
- Gear tiers: Common --> Uncommon --> Rare --> Masterwork --> Legendary
- **Legendary gear** is crafted only and carries the crafter's signature (see [[Crafting System]])
- **No gear treadmill** — Legendary gear remains viable across content updates; new tiers are not added to invalidate old gear

This separation means a player with common gear but brilliant gem configuration can outperform a player with legendary gear and a poor build. Skill expression lives in gems. Gear is the foundation, not the ceiling.

## Milestone Unlocks

| Milestone | Unlock |
|---|---|
| First gem mastery 10 | Creator profile unlocked |
| 3 builds at gem mastery 5+ each | Versatility gem slot |
| All alignments explored | Unbound specialization |
| 100 PvP wins | Arena title + exclusive aura |
| Master crafter (50 legendaries crafted) | Crafter storefront |

Milestones reward breadth of engagement. Players who explore multiple systems gain tangible benefits that deepen their options further.
